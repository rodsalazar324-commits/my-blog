## I.
Around 9:30 p.m., I find myself at the MRT station. I have to brush past others, afraid the last train will leave without me if I don’t hurry. Inside, passengers are packed like sardines in a can. Some clutch their belongings, worried they might vanish in the blink of an eye. Some are anxious about arriving at work on time. While a few are eager to return home. Yet, I'm spacing out, contemplating.

I feel less lonely when my girlfriend and I talk about random things. Usually we were talking about how to save up money, beauty of math, and what the future might hold. When the conversation turns to the future, I answer "It will be better, the future will be good to us" but in reality, I'm not sure. It's all hazy to me.

## II.

Today is my day off from work. Mathematics is my way of escaping reality. I could watch anime instead, but why math? Perhaps a part of me feels missing when I’m not learning it. I’ve been fascinated by math for as long as I can remember. Even though I took a long hiatus from it, I always find myself coming back.

Even on my day off, I can’t stop thinking about work. I remember a customer I once spoke with for nearly an hour. He was Chinese, in his fifties. For the sake of rapport, I asked how he was doing. He said he was doing well and mentioned that he was both a CPA and a computer scientist. I found his latter profession more interesting, so I asked him about it. He explained his work in great detail as if he is talking about art. While I was listening, I asked myself "Could I ever see myself as a computer scientist?" I don’t think so. I don’t like the idea of writing code or creating websites.

It has been almost two years since I dropped out of school. Do I regret it? No. Do I have plans to finish college? Definitely. I don’t love my work, but I don’t hate it either. Sure, It pays the bills, but is this what I want to be in the future? No. Even when I was a child, I never imagined I would end up here.

I love being in school, the classroom, the blackboard, the smell of chalk, and the idea of wearing uniforms. I like being in school as if there's a hope there.  The desire to graduate, to have a better life, or even to have a job that I enjoy. I don't know what job I want right now, but it should be related to math.

I'm not sure what my future holds for me. Things don't always go as planned, and I'm not sure if I'll be working in civil engineering or math major in the future. I'm not certain. But I know I miss being in a classroom.
